Project Powerglove

The theme for this year's hackathon focused on enhancing one's experience while travelling. Our glove enables bikers and electric scooter commuters to safely travel at night. This product was inspired by the increasing popularity of electric scooters as a primary form of transportation for college students. Our glove allows users to indicate which direction they are turning and whether they are braking or not using figure gestures registered by flex sensors. We've also added the functionality of being able to control the throttle of your electric vehicle using a gyroscope module to mimic a twist throttle. To increase functionality of our glove, we've added cruise control which can be activated by unique finger gestures. Although we did not move onto the next judging phase, I'm proud of how we managed ourselves as a team and the outcome of our efforts.

Components

The glove was comprised of the following electronics parts:

  • Arduino Nano
  • DC Motor
  • Accelerometer/Gyroscope/Magnetic Compass 9DOF Module (Adafruit)
  • 3x Flex Sensors
  • 2x LED Strips
  • 2x NPN Transistors
  • 2x Resistors
  • 9V Battery
  • Lots of wires!
